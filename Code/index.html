Correlations Discovered Between Health Risks and Poverty Level, Age and Household Income

Obesity appears to have a positive correlation with Poverty (i.e. the higher Poverty the higher the obesity), a negative correlation with Household Income, and no correlation with Age.

Smoking has a positive correlation with Poverty, and a slightly positive correlation with Age. But the strongest correlation appears to be a negative one with Household Income. The lower the Household Income the higher the percentage that smokes.

In terms of Lacking Healthcare Coverage, there is a slightly positive correlation with Poverty and a slightly negative correlation with Age and Household Income.
